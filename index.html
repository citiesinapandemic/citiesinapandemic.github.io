Cities in a Pandemic

A research project regarding the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic on the daily life of local students in Kuala Lumpur and Bangkok

We need your help!

We are six Austrian students from the University of Innsbruck who are interested in the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic on students in Kuala Lumpur (KL) and Bangkok (BK). Our study is part of a research project at the University of Innsbruck in Austria that deals with urban futures in Southeast Asia. There are three main objectives that we follow with our research. First, we want to get a deeper insight how the Covid-19 pandemic and the restrictions affect the daily life of students in KL and BK in general. Our second aim is to find out if and how the students’ perception and interaction with their environment has changed since the start of the pandemic. Finally, we raise the question if the crisis has led to a reassessment of personal needs regarding the city and urban space. In other words, how should future urban planning look like to deal with crisis, like a pandemic?

Overview of our Project

We are a group of Austrian students that were originally planning to travel to Kuala Lumpur/Malaysia and bangkok/Thailand to get to know city life better and to do research on urban development and planning.
Unfortunately, due to Covid-19 and the imposed travel restrictions, the trip has been cancelled. Nevertheless, we are curious about your city and everyday life in your urban environemnt. Furthermore we are interested in how Covid-19 affects you and your city. Therefore, we would like to invite you to take part in our "remote" research project! Although we are not on site to get in touch with you personally, we prepared different digital tools you can easyl use for sharing your expperiences with us. We have different activities planned so you can choose on of the following options of participation and let us know what is on your mind and what you think about urban futures.

Note: the required language is English.
